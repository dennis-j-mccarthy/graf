About the Author

Psychotherapist, author, advocate.

Vanessa Graf is a psychotherapist, author, and voice for women reclaiming autonomy in a world shaped by patriarchal systems. For more than twenty-five years she has worked in private practice, helping clients navigate trauma, relationships, identity, and the complex process of becoming fully themselves.

Her work bridges traditional psychotherapy with a broader exploration of consciousness, spirituality, and cultural conditioning. Over decades of listening to women's stories, Vanessa became increasingly interested in the invisible forces shaping women's sense of agency — from family dynamics to religion, romance, and social expectation.

These insights inspired her book 11 Steps to Leaving Your Daddy: A Radical Love Letter to Women, a bold and provocative manifesto inviting women to examine the systems that shape their lives and reclaim their sovereignty.

Vanessa's writing blends psychological insight, personal reflection, and cultural critique. Through her work she encourages readers to question inherited narratives, cultivate self-trust, and pursue lives guided by autonomy, authenticity, and emotional truth. She lives in California where she maintains a psychotherapy practice and continues writing about healing, power, and personal liberation.
